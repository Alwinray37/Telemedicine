* Tinea Cruris (Jock Rash)

            
                 
 

WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS OF OF TINEA CRURIS (JOCK RASH)?

The symptoms of Tinea Cruris (Jock Rash) are a red and itchy rash in warm and moist areas of the body. The rash often affects the groin and inner thighs-but not the scrotum.


      
 

WHAT IS THE CAUSE OF TINEA CRURIS (JOCK RASH)?

Tinea cruris is is commonly caused by a specific fungus, Trichophyton rubrum or T. mentagrophytes. Warm moist areas of the body increase the likelihood of Jock Rash.


     
 

HOW IS TINEA CRURIS (JOCK RASH) DIAGNOSED?

Tinea Cruris can be diagnosed with a careful history and a visual inspection of the skin. If there is doubt of the diagnosis, a scraping can be taken of the effected skin, a little potassium hydroxide can be mixed with a little skin, and examined under the microscope for a particular type of cells, which if present means the person has a fungus growth.


     
 

WHAT IS THE TREATMENT FOR TINEA CRURIS (JOCK RASH)?

1. Lotions:
A. Clotrimazole 2 times a day for 10 to 14 days (plus 7 days after symptoms resolve)
B. Terbinafine (Lamisil) {OTC}
C. Miconazole {OTC}
D. Ketoconazole {RX}
E. Econazole {OTC}
F. Naftifine {OTC}

1. Oral antifungals
A. Itraconazole {RX} 200 mg orally once once a day for 3 to 6 weeks
B. Terbinafine {RX} 250 mg orally each day

      
 

 

CAUTION:

      
 

1. There are several other skin conditions that look similar to Jock Rash. One of these, which is called Erythrasms, is cause by a bacteri, Corynebacterium minutissimum . It occurs most commonly in diabetics, and it usually requires either a topical antibiotic or oral antibiotic, Clarithromycin.

2. All medication, including all anti-fungal medications, have a risk of side effects.

3. Before using a new lotion or cream on a large area of skin, try it on a small patch of skin.

4. Possible mild side effects are nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, itching, and light to moderate skin rash.

5. Possible severe side effects are a severe skin rash, liver damage, or an anaphylactic allergic reaction (leading to the inability to breathe and subsequent death if not treated rapidly).
