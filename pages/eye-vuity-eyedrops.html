WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS OF PRESBYOPIA AND HYPEROPIA?

The symptom of Presbyopia is the slow decrease in the ability to see close object over many years. It usually starts after 50 years.

Hyperopia symptoms is the inability see close objects at a young age, sometimes starting at birth.




     
 

WHAT IS THE CAUSE OF PRESBYOPIA AND HYPEROPIA?

Presbyopia occurs because the lens of the eye becomes less flexible as a person ages. Therefore, the lens is less able to change its shape like it should to direct light to the macula. The macula is part of the retina that is responsible for central vision and fine details of sight.

Hyperopia has two possible causes. One reason is there is a defect in the cornea. The second reason is the eyeball is abnormally short. The effect of both of these eye defects is that light rays do not focus on the proper part of the retina (macula), which is necessary for sharp clear eyesight.




     
 

HOW ARE PRESBYOPIA AND HYPEROPIA DIAGNOSED?

Presbyopia and hyperopia can be made by taking a careful history and by making a brief eye exam.




      
 

WHAT IS THE TREATMENT FOR PRESBYOPIA AND HYPEROPIA?

1. The best treatment for both presbyopia and hyperopia is glasses, commonly called reading glasses.

2. “Vuity Eye Drops” are another option. They may allow a person to see close objects much better. These drops work best for mild presbyopia or hyperopia. They are not recommended for people with severe presbyopia and severe hyperopia.

     
 

 

CAUTION:

      
 

1. Vuity in RARE circumstances can cause the retina to detach from the back of the eye (vitreous cavity); people with bad presbyopia are more likely to have this happen than those who have mild presbyopia.

2. As with any medications, there are always some risk of side effects.

3. When trying a new eye medication it is best to try only one drop in one eye the first time used.
