FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTION?
  
 

1. What is telemedicine and how does it work?

Telemedicine is a system of providing medical care via the use of telephones (primarily cellular phones) or the Internet. Telemedicine allows certain licensed health care providers to evaluate, diagnose, and treat patients from either short or long distances without leaving their homes.


2. What is the difference bwteeen telemedicine and telehealth?

Telemedicine is a method to give medical services via a cell phone or the Internet. These services include evlauating the patient, ordering test(s), making a diagnosis and ordering medicine(s).

Telehealth is a broader term than telemedicine. Telehealth includes telemedicine, but it also includes providing health training lectures and videos to health care providers and more. Telehealth also includes electronic surveillance of certain health conditions. Examples of electronic telehealth surveillance are the surveillance of a patient's blood pressure, their electrocardiograms, and their glucose levels.


3. What do I need to know about telemedicine?

Telemedicine is very useful for minor illnessness that do not require an in-person examination. Examples of these are skin conditions, urinary tract infections, and sore throats. However, telemedicine is not good for illnesses such as lacerations, broken bones, breathing problems, heart arrhythmias, and high fevers.


4. Where can I find telemedicine services?

You can find telemedicine services by using the Internet and just searching for "telemedicine" in your state (Arizona, Nevada, etc.). If you have insurance, you can also call your insurance company and ask if they have telemedicine services. Their telephone number is always on the back of their card. In addition, if you have a primary care physician, you can call him or her and ask if they have telemedicine services.


5. Is it better to see a doctor in his office or via telemedicine?

Generally, it is better for you to see a doctor in person rather than use telemedicine services. In the doctor's office the physician can examine you more thoroughly, and do tests that he could not do when you are at home. However, if you have an infectious disease such as COVID 19 or the flu, and you go into the doctor's office you can spread your illness to anyone you come in contact with. Masks reduce this possibility--but do not eliminate it.


6. How do I book a telemedicine visit?

First, find a telemedicine website that offers services for your state (Arizona, Nevada, etc.) It can be an independent provider, your primary care physicians office, or your insurance companies recommended telemedicine provider. Then you will usually have to fill out a breif form on line and select a date and a time for your appointment. Sometimes, you may be able to this by calling your desired party and requesting a telemedicine visit verbally.


7. Are telemedicine services available 7 days a week?

Some telemedicine services are available 7 days a week, however, not all are. Each telemedicine providers has the ability to control their own hours of availability.


8. What are the benefits of telemedince?

One of the wonderful benefits of telemedine is that it requires less time and less effort. A patient can be seen from their home, their work place, or even their school. The patient does not have to waste time traveling back and forth to their doctor's office. In addition, the patient does not risk exposing the doctor's office staff, or the doctor, or other patients in the doctor's office with their illness. This is expecially important in regards to COVID 19.


9. What medical problems can be treated by telemedine?

In general, most mild to moderate illnessness can be treated with telemedicine. Some common illnesses that can be treated with telemedicine are the flu, COVID 19, urinay tract infections, and most skin problems.


10. What medical disorders can not be treated with telemedicine?

Although there are many mild to moderate medical illnesses that can treated with telemedicine, there are hundreds more that must be seen in person. In general, any severe or rapidly progressing illnesses should not be seen by telemedicine. The more severe the symptoms are the more quickly they need to be seen.

As a general rule patient who have any severe pain should be seen in person. Severe pain can be a sign of many bad medical problems such as a brain aneurysm, heart attack, or appendicities.

Anyone having difficulty breathing or anyone who is short of breath should be seen in person. They could have COVID 19, pneumonia, asthma, or COPD.

Anyone having irregular heart rates or palpitation or who has fainting spells have to be seen in person. An irregular pulse rate could lead to a fatal heart attack or stroke.
