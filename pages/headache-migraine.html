WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS OF A COMMON HEADACHE?

Usually a common headache comes on slow and it hurts equally on both sides of the head.Slow onset. The pain is usually dull but it sometimes feels like a band around the head. Usually the pain involves the back part of the head or neck. And usually describe the pain as moderate—not severe.

 

      
 

WHAT IS THE CAUSE OF A COMMON HEADACHE?

The exact cause of a common headache unknown. What is known is that a headache occurs from a complex interaction between nerves and blood vessels. This complex interaction can be started by a variety of factors. These factors include the following: stress, lack of sleep, fatigue, hunger, certain foods and drinks (such as chocolate), caffeine withdrawal, abrupt cessation of medications that contain caffeine, and alcohol withdrawal.


      
 

HOW IS A HEADACHE DIAGNOSED?

The diagnosis of headache can be made by a careful history and a brief physical exam.


      
 

HOW IS A HEADACHE TREATED?

1. Acetaminophen (Tylenol) as needed. Provided the person does not have any liver problems.
2. Ibuprofen (Motrin or Advil) Provided the patient has no stomach or kidney problems and is not in the third term of pregnancy.


      
 

 

CAUTION:

      


1. Sometimes a headache can be an emergency. An emergency head has some of the following symptoms.

A. A headache that comes on suddenly and is very severe
B. A headache that feels sharp and severe, which a person has never felt before.
C. A headache accompanied by a stiff neck and fever.
D. A headache with a high fever (102 to 104°F).
E. A headache with nausea and vomiting.
F. A headache with a nosebleed.
G. A headache with fainting.
H. A headache with dizziness or loss of balance.

2. Make sure it is not better diagnosed as a Migraine.

WHAT ARE THE SYMPTOMS OF A MIGRAINE?

1. Migraine headache pain is severe throbbing or pulsing pain.
2. The migraine headache is usually accompanied by nausea and vomiting,
3. Migraine headaches pain is usually accompanied by extreme sensitivity to light or sound.
4. Migraine headaches usually last from 4 to 72 hours
5. Migraine headaches are usually made worse by activity, bright lights, and loud noises.

 

      
 

WHAT IS THE CAUSE OF A MIGRAINE?

The cause of a migraine is poorly understood. One theory states that it is a combination of a nervous system problems and a blood vessel problem. However, this theory is not accepted by everyone.

 

      
 

HOW IS A MIGRAINE DIAGNOSED?

A diagnosis of a Migraine can usually be made by a careful history and a brief neurological exam.

Caution:

1. There are certain symptoms that if they occur indicate that headache is more than a headache and requires a more extensive assessment as soon as possible. Some of these symptoms are the following:
A. Sudden, very intense headache pain.

2. All medication, including medications those for migraines, have a risk of side effects. Many migraine medication work by constricting blood vessels in the head; however, the constriction does not stop there. They may also constrict some blood vessels that to the heart, which increases a person’s chance of having a heart attack.
A. Possible mild side effects are nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, itching, and light skin rash.
B. Possible severe side effects are a severe rash or an anaphylactic allergic reaction (leading to the inability to breathe and subsequent death if not treated rapidly).
